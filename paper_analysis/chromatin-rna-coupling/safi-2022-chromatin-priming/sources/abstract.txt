Concurrent stem- and lineage-affiliated chromatin programs precede hematopoietic 
lineage restriction.

Erratum in
    Cell Rep. 2023 Oct 31;42(10):113357. doi: 10.1016/j.celrep.2023.113357.

The emerging notion of hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells (HSPCs) as a 
low-primed cloud without sharply demarcated gene expression programs raises the 
question on how cellular-fate options emerge and at which stem-like stage 
lineage priming is initiated. Here, we investigate single-cell chromatin 
accessibility of Lineage-, cKit+, and Sca1+ (LSK) HSPCs spanning the early 
differentiation landscape. Application of a signal-processing algorithm to 
detect transition points corresponding to massive alterations in accessibility 
of 571 transcription factor motifs reveals a population of LSK FMS-like tyrosine 
kinase 3 (Flt3)intCD9high cells that concurrently display stem-like and 
lineage-affiliated chromatin signatures, pointing to a simultaneous gain of both 
lympho-myeloid and megakaryocyte-erythroid programs. Molecularly and 
functionally, these cells position between stem cells and committed progenitors 
and display multi-lineage capacity in vitro and in vivo but lack self-renewal 
activity. This integrative molecular analysis resolves the heterogeneity of 
cells along hematopoietic differentiation and permits investigation of 
chromatin-mediated transition between multipotency and lineage restriction.

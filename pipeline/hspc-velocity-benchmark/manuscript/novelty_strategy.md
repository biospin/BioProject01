## 1. Q1 — Does the scoop leave a real paper? (adversarial, then rule)

**The threat (MoFlow, Hong et al. 2025):** reports "a consistent subset of genes exhibiting negative c–s
lags via both global time from MultiVelo and MoFlow." I.e. a published, peer-reviewed cross-method
per-gene lag comparison already exists, and it reports *agreement*.

**Argue INCREMENTAL (steelman the reviewer):**
- The raw phenomenon — two chromatin-informed methods give partly different per-gene lags — is already in
  the literature. Adding a permutation-FDR test and two more arms is "we looked harder and found less
  agreement": a quantitative tightening, not a new kind of finding.
- Our own empty agreement-set, as computed, leans on an arm we call buggy (§0). Strip that and we are left
  with three methods at ρ ≈ 0 — still just "concordance is low," which a critic frames as re-stating
  MoFlow's caveat that MultiVelo "imposes overly linear trajectory dependencies."
- Pure-concordance papers are hard to place: "no universal winner" is already the headline of two 2026
  velocity benchmarks (Cell Reports Methods 2026; bioRxiv 697314).

**Argue GENUINE ADVANCE:**
- MoFlow's "consistent subset" is a **post-hoc favorable-subset validation** (the genes where its method
  concurs), with **no null model, no FDR, no held-out method, and no causal control.** It never tested
  whether that subset is reproducible or chromatin-driven. We falsify the *implicit* claim that a
  reproducible concordant core exists.
- The **causal ATAC-shuffle negative control is categorically new and scoop-proof.** Shuffling ATAC
  within lineage leaves MultiVelo's lag statistically unchanged (MW p=0.20, KS p=0.51, per-gene ρ=0.72
  preserved, chromatin likelihood 0.239→0.237). That is a **mechanistic** claim — the lag is generated by
  switch-time ordering, not chromatin — not a "methods disagree" claim. No competing paper (MoFlow
  included) runs this test. A concordance number is a competitive claim; a null-model + causal control is
  a different species of claim.
- The **α-robust / lag-fragile dissociation** is a *constructive positive* absent from every method paper:
  the identical pipeline yields a highly reproducible α (cross-method ρ=**0.88**) and a non-reproducible
  lag, and the same day0-ATAC baseline predicts α (held-out ρ=+0.31, 6/6 lineages positive) but not lag
  (≈chance). "What survives the method swap vs what doesn't" is the science; MoFlow reports lag as a
  headline biological readout with no reproducibility audit at all.

**RULE — GO.** A pure "concordance is low" paper would be incremental over MoFlow and thin for venue. The
paper is real **because the load-bearing novelty is the causal control + the α invariant, not the
concordance number.** Lead with the mechanism (lag is model-structural — proven by the shuffle) and the
invariant (α is what reproduces); use the concordance as the setup, not the punchline. Framed that way,
MoFlow becomes a *cite-and-differentiate* (their agreement claim is exactly the thing our null +
held-out + shuffle tests dismantle), not a scoop.

## 2. Q2 — THE differentiated contribution (one framing, committed)

**Primary framing (commit, do not hedge): a robustness/mechanism audit of chromatin-informed velocity —
"the per-gene lag is model-structural, not chromatin-driven; α is the reproducible invariant."**
This is the (a)benchmark + (b)positive-reframe hybrid, ordered mechanism-first.

**The single sharpest publishable claim (headline sentence, ordered to put the scoop-proof asset first):**
> The gene-level chromatin→transcription lag reported by chromatin-informed velocity methods is a
> model-structural artifact — non-reproducible across methods and unchanged when the chromatin↔RNA
> coupling is destroyed by shuffling ATAC — whereas the transcription rate α is the cross-method invariant
> (ρ=0.88); timing-prediction models should be built on α (recoverable from baseline day0 ATAC), not the lag.

**Why this framing beats the alternatives:**
- **vs (a-pure) "methods disagree/benchmark":** disagreement alone is incremental over MoFlow and the two
  2026 benchmarks. Anchoring on the *causal control* (why they disagree — the lag isn't chromatin) makes
  it mechanistic and scoop-proof.
- **vs (b-pure) "α is the robust invariant":** true and constructive, but α=0.88 alone reads as
  "transcription rate is easy to estimate" — not surprising enough to headline. It is the **contrast**
  (α robust *while* lag is a chromatin-independent artifact) that carries weight. Keep α as the
  constructive second half, not the lead. **Wording guard:** headline the *reproducibility* (ρ=0.88);
  present the day0-ATAC→α predictive path (ρ=+0.31) as *promising-but-modest*, not established — a
  reviewer who sees 0.31 sold as "the signal to use" will discount the whole paper.
- **vs (c) "chromatin-potential fails at gene resolution" (biology paper):** demote and keep narrow. At
  gene resolution this is defensible only as "the *global per-gene, method-invariant* chromatin-leads
  ordering is unsupported in HSPC" — canonical priming markers *do* lead, and our own data agree. Pitched
  as a biology headline it is directly attackable by the Ma et al. 2020 chromatin-potential line and
  overreaches beyond our two datasets. It is a one-paragraph implication, not the thesis.
- **vs (d) "methods-caution/guidance":** this is the *consequence* section (drug-timing models must not
  consume a single-method lag; use α / day0-ATAC→α), not the contribution. Guidance without the causal
  mechanism is an opinion piece.

**Venue class:** methods-oriented audit — **Genome Biology** or **Cell Reports Methods** (best fit:
critique+benchmark+constructive-invariant with a causal control). Bioinformatics as fallback. **Not** a
primary biology venue (the biology negative is narrow and Ma-attackable). Note: a GB/CRM-class audit with
**n=1 dataset is thin** — the cross-dataset replication (§4.3) is load-bearing for the venue, not optional.

## 3. Q3 — Confronting MoFlow's "consistent subset" (the sharpest-threat experiment)

> **⚠️ 갱신 2026-07-05 — 이 절의 예측은 실측으로 부분 falsify됨. 아래 원문 대신 이 결론을 쓸 것.**
> 실험(트랙 A, `results/moflow_subset_confrontation.md`) 결과: subset S(60 gene)은 held-out MVVAE에서 **+12.4pp chromatin-leading enrichment(p=0.037, n=60, uncorrected, mirror 대칭)** → "held-out에 일반화 안 됨"은 **틀렸다**(약한 방향성 concordance 실재). 단 magnitude rank ρ≈0.10–0.15, S 내 agreement-set 0/60, ATAC-shuffle에서 lag *순서* chromatin-독립(rank ρ=0.70≈전유전체 0.72) → chromatin-priming 해석은 붕괴. **정직한 최종 주장 = "약한 방향 일치는 있으나 재현되는 per-gene 크기 없음 + chromatin 인과 없음(switch-time 구조적)."** manuscript-writer는 이 판정을 쓴다. (원문은 설계 근거로만 보존.)

**The trap to avoid (circularity):** MoFlow's consistent subset is *defined* as the genes where
MoFlow + MultiVelo agree. Re-measuring MoFlow/MultiVelo agreement on that selected set is a pure selection
effect and proves nothing. The test must use methods **not used to select the subset.**

**Non-circular design (held-out concordance + causal control):**

1. **Reconstruct the subset on its own two methods.** In our GSE209878 data, define
   `S = {genes where MultiVelo lag < 0 AND MoFlow c–s lag < 0}` (both chromatin-leading / negative-lag) —
   the operationalization of MoFlow's "consistent subset." Record |S|.
2. **Held-out concordance (the discriminating test).** Measure, *on S only*, concordance with the methods
   that did **not** define S:
   - **MultiVeloVAE** (clean held-out arm) — Spearman ρ of |lag| and sign-agreement %.
   - **CRAK-Velo** — sensitivity only, reported with its bug caveat.
   Compare S-concordance to the genome-wide concordance from `permutation_fdr.md`.
   **Prediction that discriminates the two hypotheses:**
   - *If S is real biological signal* → held-out methods (MVVAE) should show **elevated** concordance on S
     vs genome-wide (S enriches for reproducible genes).
   - *If S is a 2-method sign coincidence selected post-hoc* → MVVAE concordance on S is **≈ chance /
     ≈ genome-wide** (no enrichment). Given moflow×multivelovae is already +0.08 / 48% genome-wide, the
     expected result is **no enrichment → S does not generalize.**
3. **Causal control on S.** Run the ATAC-shuffle (`p3_scrambled_null.py`) restricted to S: does MultiVelo's
   lag on S change when chromatin is scrambled? **Prediction:** preserved (as genome-wide, ρ≈0.72) → even
   the "consistent" subset's lag is **structural, not chromatin-driven.** This is the killer line: MoFlow's
   favorable subset is not evidence of chromatin priming; it is where two switch-time orderings happen to
   agree, and it survives having chromatin removed.
4. **FDR on S.** Re-run the per-gene sign-consistency FDR (`permutation_fdr.md` §B logic) restricted to S
   with the clean methods. **Prediction:** agreement-set on S stays ≈ empty under held-out + null.

**One-line result the paper can claim (if predictions hold):** *MoFlow's "consistent subset" does not
generalize to a held-out method and its lag is preserved under ATAC shuffling — the apparent agreement is
a two-method structural coincidence, not reproducible chromatin-driven timing.* That sentence converts the
sharpest scoop threat into a supporting result.

**Cost:** near-zero new compute — reuses the four existing per-gene lag tables + `p3_scrambled_null.py` +
the `permutation_fdr.py` null machinery. Effort ≈ 1 day of re-analysis. **This IS the cheapest high-value
experiment (see §4.1).**

## 5. Honest verdict

**The current framing is novel enough to publish, but needs a re-order, not a re-scope.** It is not
crowded and it is not scooped — MoFlow anticipates the *observation* of partial cross-method lag
agreement, not the *mechanism* (causal ATAC-shuffle) or the *invariant* (α). The failure mode to avoid is
leading with the concordance number (incremental over MoFlow) instead of the causal control (scoop-proof).

**Single highest-leverage next move:** run **§4.1 (MoFlow-subset held-out confrontation)** immediately
after the §4.0 clean re-run — it is ~1 day of re-analysis on assets already computed, and it is the exact
evidence that turns the paper's sharpest external threat into one of its supporting results. Then lock the
central claim by executing §4.2 (multi-method causal control) as the flagship confirmatory run.

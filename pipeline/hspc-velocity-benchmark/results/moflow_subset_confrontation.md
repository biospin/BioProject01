# MoFlow "consistent subset" held-out confrontation (2026-07-05)

> novelty_strategy §3/§4.1 (#1 experiment). MoFlow (Hong et al. 2025) reports a "consistent subset" of genes whose
> chromatin→spliced lag agrees between MoFlow and MultiVelo. This is the sharpest scoop threat. We test whether that
> subset is real signal or a 2-method selection artifact, using methods that **did not** define it + a causal control.
> Pure re-analysis of existing lag tables + `scrambled_genes.csv` (ATAC-shuffle refit). No new fits.

## Sign-convention landmine (fixed before defining S)
novelty_strategy §3 copied MoFlow's wording `S = {MultiVelo lag < 0 AND MoFlow c-s lag < 0}`. Two errors:
1. MultiVelo lag is **100% positive** (`crakvelo_sign_check.md`) → `MultiVelo lag < 0` is the **empty set**.
2. In the codebase `cs_lag_median` column, **chromatin-leading = positive** (myeloid markers AZU1/PRTN3/CTSG/ELANE/MPO
   all +) — the **opposite** of the MoFlow paper's "negative c-s lag." Implementing `< 0` would select the exact
   inverse of MoFlow's subset.

## S definition (defensible operationalization)
MoFlow's "consistent subset" = genes where MoFlow and MultiVelo **agree on chromatin-leading with large |lag|**.
MultiVelo is structurally chromatin-leading (all +), so agreement reduces to **MoFlow cs_lag > 0**, plus a
magnitude gate in both:

> **S = { MoFlow cs_lag_median > 0  AND  |MoFlow lag| ≥ median(0.143)  AND  MultiVelo lag ≥ median(5.864) }**

|shared moflow∩multivelo| = 537 → **|S| = 60 genes**. (MultiVelo's contribution is near-trivial; S is effectively
MoFlow-selected — so the truly held-out method is **MultiVeloVAE**, which never participated.)

## Held-out concordance on S (methods that did NOT define S)

| test on S (n=60) | on S | genome-wide | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MVVAE chromatin-leads (>0)** (held-out) | **61.7%** (37/60) | 49.3% | **+12.4 pp** |
| moflow×mvvae sign-agreement | 61.7% | 48.1% | +13.6 pp |
| moflow×mvvae Spearman(signed lag) | +0.104 | +0.083 | +0.021 |
| \|MVVAE\| vs MultiVelo magnitude rank | +0.148 | — | — |
| CRAK-Velo chromatin-leads (>0) on S (sensitivity) | 30.6% | 41.1% | −10.5 pp (opposite) |

The held-out MVVAE enrichment (61.7% vs 49.3%) is **modest and only nominal**: one-sided binomial p=**0.037**
(two-sided 0.070), n=60, **uncorrected**. So it is a **weak sign-*direction* tendency, not strong reproducibility** —
and the **magnitude** rank concordance stays near-zero (+0.10 to +0.15), i.e. *which* gene has the largest lag does
not reproduce even inside S. The 4th method (CRAK) does not concur (goes the other way, 30.6%).

**Symmetry check — genuine weak concordance, not a magnitude-selection artifact.** Build the mirror subset
**M = {MoFlow cs_lag < 0 (RNA-leading), |MoFlow|≥median, MultiVelo≥median}** (n=76). Held-out MVVAE is enriched for
**RNA-leading (<0) at 61.8%** on M (genome-wide 50.7%; one-sided binom p=0.033) — the mirror image of S. Because
the enrichment **flips direction with MoFlow's sign** (not MVVAE>0 regardless), it is genuine weak directional
concordance between MoFlow and the held-out MVVAE concentrated in the large-|lag| tails, **not** an
α_c/magnitude-gate selection artifact. This is why the verdict says "not a pure 2-method coincidence" — but the
concordance is *directional and weak only*, and (below) it is not chromatin-driven.

## Per-gene agreement-set FDR restricted to S
`{moflow, mvvae}` on S: **0/60**. `{moflow, crakvelo, mvvae}` on S: **0/60**. No gene in the "consistent subset"
reaches method-invariant sign consistency above the sign-shuffle null.

## Causal control — ATAC-shuffle on S (the decisive test)
Restricting the within-lineage ATAC-shuffle refit (`scrambled_genes.csv`) to S:

- **per-gene rank preserved**: Spearman(original MV lag, chromatin-scrambled MV lag) on S = **+0.703**
  (genome-wide +0.721) — the *ordering* of which genes lead is chromatin-independent even inside the subset.
  (S is range-restricted to MultiVelo ≥ median, which only attenuates ρ → true preservation ≥ 0.70, conservative.)
- **small but significant magnitude reduction**: paired Wilcoxon on S p=**2.5e-5**, median **7.79 → 7.67** (−1.5%).
  Apples-to-apples, the genome-wide paired Wilcoxon is p=3e-4, median 5.87 → 5.47 (−6.8%) — so the magnitude
  reduction on S is significant but **no larger than (in absolute terms smaller than) genome-wide.** Chromatin
  contributes only marginally to lag *magnitude*, on S as genome-wide.

**Reading:** even inside MoFlow's "consistent subset," the per-gene lag *ordering* is chromatin-independent
(rank ρ=0.70 preserved under shuffle), with only a marginal magnitude contribution from chromatin — i.e. the
subset's lag is generated by switch-time ordering, not chromatin priming. (Not "unchanged"; rank-invariant with a
small significant magnitude shift.)

## Verdict
**The "consistent subset" is not a pure 2-method coincidence, but its chromatin-priming interpretation collapses.**
A held-out method (MVVAE) shows a *weak, nominal* above-chance chromatin-leading tendency on S (61.7% vs 49.3%,
one-sided p≈0.04, uncorrected), and the mirror subset confirms this is genuine (symmetric) directional concordance
between MoFlow and MVVAE in the large-|lag| tails — so we honestly report weak ≠ zero. But (a) the per-gene
*magnitude* stays non-reproducible even inside S (ρ≈0.10–0.15), (b) the per-gene agreement-set on S is empty under
FDR (0/60), (c) the 4th method (CRAK) does not concur, and **decisively (d) the causal control shows S's lag
*ordering* is chromatin-independent — per-gene rank preserved under ATAC shuffle (ρ=0.70 ≈ genome-wide 0.72), with
only a marginal magnitude reduction (paired Wilcoxon p=2.5e-5, −1.5%, no larger than genome-wide).** So MoFlow's
favorable subset reflects a *weak directional* concordance that is **switch-time-structural, not chromatin-driven,
and not reproducible in per-gene magnitude.** The sharpest scoop threat converts into a supporting result: the
"consistent subset" does not carry reproducible per-gene timing and its lag ordering survives destroying chromatin.

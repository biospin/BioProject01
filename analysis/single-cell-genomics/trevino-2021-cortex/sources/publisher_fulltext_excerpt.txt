Article: Chromatin and gene-regulatory dynamics of the developing human cerebral cortex at single-cell resolution
Journal: Cell, Volume 184, Issue 19, 16 September 2021, Pages 5053-5069.e23

Highlights:
- Single-cell RNA and chromatin profiling charts human corticogenesis.
- Distinct TFs underlie neurogenesis and gliogenesis regulatory programs.
- Lineage-determining TFs adopt an active chromatin state early in differentiation.
- Neural networks prioritize noncoding de novo mutations in autism spectrum disorder.

Summary / source boundary note:
The page states that the study mapped gene-regulatory element activity to generate a single-cell atlas of gene expression and chromatin accessibility in developing human cortex, using independent and joint measurements. It reports TF-driven regulatory waves along a near-continuous differentiation trajectory, glial lineage expression programs, strong correlation between lineage-determining TF expression and linked regulatory elements, early active chromatin state adoption in differentiating cells, and BP-resolution neural network prioritization of disruptive ASD noncoding mutations.

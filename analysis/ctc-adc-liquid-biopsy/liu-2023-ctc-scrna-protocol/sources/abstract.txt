Protocol for identifying immune checkpoint on circulating tumor cells of human 
pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma by single-cell RNA sequencing.

Circulating tumor cells (CTCs) are regarded as the "seeds" of tumor metastasis. 
Identifying immune checkpoints on CTCs is essential for establishing efficient 
immunotherapies to prevent tumor metastasis. Here, we present a protocol for 
isolating CTCs and obtaining single-cell suspensions from pancreatic ductal 
adenocarcinoma liver metastatic patients. We describe steps for biospecimen 
acquisition, CTC isolation, and tissue dissociation. We then detail procedures 
for performing single-cell RNA-seq, annotating cell types, and identifying 
immune checkpoints on CTCs. For complete details on the use and execution of 
this protocol, please refer to Liu et al. (2023).1.
